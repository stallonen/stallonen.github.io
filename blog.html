$$ \huge \text{I might discuss about}\hspace 1pt \pi \hspace 1pt \text{here}.$$ $$$$ \(\huge\text{On identity elements of algebraic structures.}\) $$$$ A question that has been eating at me for a few days is, why do we need an identity element for certain algebraic structures like groups, rings, fields?
Say, we consider the structure \((\mathbb{N},+)\). It fails to be an abelian group because it lacks an identity element. Hence an additive inverse.
However it might be considered a loop. Is it just that we're considering different structures?
Perhaps an argument is that, we're studying different structures and group is just a structure equipped with an identity element.
